Economic Strengths

  • GDP growth maintains resilience at 2.8% annually despite elevated rates
  • Labor market retains fundamental strength with unemployment at 4.2%
  • Financial system health solid with NPL ratio at manageable 1.7%
  • Consumer spending supported by wage growth of 3.9% annually
  • Core inflation showing gradual deceleration toward Fed 2% target
  • Corporate balance sheets generally healthy with strong capital ratios
  • Dollar maintains reserve currency status providing external financing flexibility

Economic Concerns

  • Federal deficit at $1.8 trillion represents 6.4% of GDP during expansion
  • Public debt-to-GDP ratio elevated at 121% with rising trajectory
  • Trade deficit widens to record $1.2 trillion creating external vulnerabilities
  • Labor force participation declining to 62.2%, lowest since November 2022
  • Housing affordability crisis persists with elevated prices and mortgage rates
  • Geopolitical tensions creating trade policy uncertainty and supply chain risks
  • Interest burden consuming increasing share of federal budget
ECONOMIC OUTLOOK

Near-Term Trajectory: The US economy is positioned for continued moderate growth through remainder of 2025, though at potentially slower pace than earlier in year. Deceleration in employment growth, rising unemployment, and tighter financial conditions suggest economic momentum moderating in response to sustained Federal Reserve policy restraint.

Inflation Dynamics: Recent uptick in headline inflation to 2.7% complicates Federal Reserve decision-making and may delay anticipated easing of monetary policy. However, underlying disinflationary forces from moderating demand and improved supply chains suggest pressures may continue to moderate over time.

Medium-Term Challenges: Fiscal trajectory with deficits exceeding 6% of GDP during expansion suggests unsustainable debt dynamics. Demographic trends and declining labor force participation present long-term growth constraints requiring productivity improvements and policy responses.

Risk Factors: Key risks include fiscal sustainability concerns, external financing requirements, potential monetary policy missteps, and ongoing geopolitical tensions affecting trade relationships and supply chains.
